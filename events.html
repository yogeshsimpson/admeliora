Clean in Montana

Learning Clean Language can change how you think, talk, and collaborate. Three days in the Montana spring, working with questions that get out of their own way.

What to expect

Would you like to get better at working with others?

Clean Language is a way of asking questions that keeps your own assumptions out of someone else's thinking. It sounds simple. It changes everything about how a team listens, decides, and moves together.

Over three days, you'll learn the core questions, practice them on real material, and leave with a way of working you can bring straight back to your team, your clients, or your own household.

Who it's for

  • Coaches who want a sharper, cleaner questioning practice
  • Leaders and facilitators who run rooms full of strong opinions
  • Anyone curious about how language shapes what a group can see
